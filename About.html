Distant Horizon

About

Gameplay

Distant Horizon is a game about flying a trading spaceship in a realistic universe. It is hard science-fiction; the future of Distant Horizon requires no massive leaps in technology. What is represented is one of humanity's possible futures. Many space games seem to use atmospheric, airplane physics. Others use naval combat models. Distant Horizon is an attempt to demonstrate what realistic space travel would really be like, and to show that you don't have to diverge from reality for interesting space gameplay to emerge. Compared to the exciting futures presented in science-fiction, the true possibilities of our future sometimes seem dull. Distant Horizon is an attempt to demonstrate that the true future will be just as exciting as the fictional.

World

Distant Horizon is set in the fictional Regalis Deep, a cluster of six star systems that slowly orbit each other. This region of space was colonized by human generation ships departing from earth around 2700. Minimal contact has been had with Earth since that time. No sentient aliens are known to exist; while Regalis Deep features two naturally habitable planets, on neither has any life evolved higher intelligence. Humans have colonized these two worlds and have terraformed many more.

The planets of Regalis Deep are close enough together to allow travel between them to take place in weeks or months. This has allowed interplanetary trade to develop, and the space-lanes between planets are navigated by an active merchant fleet.

The colonization ships dispatched to Regalis were primarily funded by the Federated States of North America, the People's Republic of Greater China, and the Union of South Asian Nations. While a few planets have achieved internal planetwide government, no stable interplanetary government has emerged. A few attempts have been made, but because communication between planets takes weeks at best, only very loose alliances can be formed. While some governments are more powerful than others, no single nation is dominant.


The Code

The game client is written in Godot Engine, compiling to WebAssembly, with a multiplayer server written in Kotlin. A small web server application, written in Python/Flask, serves this static site.

All of Distant Horizon's code, except for certain key configuration files, is open sourced under GPL-3. We welcome contributions! If you choose to create a separate product based on Distant Horizon, please link your users back to this page to provide attribution.
